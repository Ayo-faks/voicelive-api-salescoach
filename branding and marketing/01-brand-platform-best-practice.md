# Wulo Brand Platform Best Practice

## Purpose

This document is the structured best-practice version of the campaign conversation. It turns the raw strategic discussion into clear messaging guidance that can be used across landing pages, LinkedIn, webinars, demos, outreach, and brand storytelling.

## Executive Summary

The strongest campaign territory for Wulo is:

**Progress Shouldn't Depend on Privilege**

This works because it keeps the emotional access story while protecting the therapist as the hero.

The campaign should not say:

- therapy is now cheap;
- anyone can replace a therapist;
- AI makes therapists less necessary.

The campaign should say:

- too much progress is still shaped by time, postcode, waiting lists, parent confidence, and continuity gaps;
- therapists remain the expert decision-makers;
- Wulo helps therapist-led care travel further between live sessions.

## Core Strategic Decision

Do not position Wulo as affordable therapy.

Position Wulo as affordable continuity, better reach, and more accessible therapist-led support between sessions.

That distinction matters.

Affordable therapy sounds like category disruption against therapists.

Affordable continuity sounds like leverage for therapists.

## Message Architecture

## The Problem

Children do not only lose progress inside a waiting list or between appointments.

They lose momentum in the silence after the session ends.

That silence is shaped by:

- scarce specialist time;
- unclear home follow-through;
- uneven family capacity;
- low therapist visibility between sessions;
- school and service constraints.

## The Hero

The therapist is the hero.

Not the app.

Not AI.

Not the avatar.

The therapist sets goals, chooses targets, interprets progress, and decides next steps.

## Wulo's Role

Wulo is the practice and review layer that helps therapist-led care continue between live sessions.

## The Outcome

More children can keep moving between appointments with structured guidance, clearer follow-through, and more visible progress, without diluting therapist control.

## Brand Manifesto

Progress in speech work is not only shaped by what happens in the session.

It is shaped by what happens after.

In the week between appointments.

In the long wait before support begins.

In homes where parents want to help but do not know exactly how.

In schools where children need more continuity than the system can easily provide.

Too often, progress depends on time, location, confidence, and whether support can be recreated outside the live session.

It should not.

Children do not need less therapist involvement. They need therapist-led continuity to reach further.

Therapists do not need to dilute their standards to support more children. They need their expertise to travel better between appointments.

Wulo exists to help care keep working when the session ends.

Guided practice for the child.

Clearer follow-through for the family.

Structured review for the therapist.

Always supervised. Never replacing judgment.

Because progress should not belong only to the children with the most support around them.

## Best-Practice Messaging Rules

## Always Do This

1. Lead with continuity, not novelty.
2. Frame access as reach, follow-through, and better support between appointments.
3. Keep the therapist visibly in control.
4. Use practical workflow proof: assign, practice, review, plan.
5. Show empathy for waiting-list families without implying self-serve therapy.
6. Talk about what breaks down after the session ends.
7. Use calm, credible, non-hype language.

## Never Lead With This

1. Cheap speech therapy
2. Therapy for everyone without a therapist
3. AI therapist
4. Replace home programs with automation
5. Eliminate the therapist bottleneck
6. Anyone can now do speech therapy

## Channel Guidance

## LinkedIn

Lead with founder point of view, therapist empathy, and category education.

Best messages:

- why the week between matters;
- why homework is not the same as structured practice;
- why families need structure, not more pressure;
- why therapists need visibility, not more raw data.

## Landing Page

Use the broad moral story in the hero, then collapse quickly into therapist workflow proof.

## Webinar

Lead with a real therapist workflow problem. Teach first. Demo second.

## Sales Deck

Use the brand promise to open, but keep the body of the deck operational and workflow-based.

## Final Strategic Test

Before publishing any message, ask:

1. Does this make the therapist feel more effective?
2. Does this protect therapist judgment and authority?
3. Does this frame access as continuity, not disintermediation?
4. Does this sound calm, credible, and category-building?

If the answer is no to any of those, revise the message.
