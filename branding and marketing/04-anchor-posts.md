# Wulo Anchor Posts

## Purpose

Three cornerstone LinkedIn posts that the rest of the 30-day campaign orbits. Each one is publish-ready, pinnable, and designed to stand on its own as a founder-voice artefact.

They map to the three lead rungs of the therapist value ladder:

1. **Leverage** — the emotional anchor.
2. **Continuity** — the clinical resonance point.
3. **Mission** — the moral close.

Use them as pinned posts, homepage quotes, sales-deck epigraphs, and webinar opens. Every other campaign post should be recognisable as a descendant of one of these three.

---

## Anchor Post 1 — Leverage

**Rung:** 1 — Professional leverage.

**Spine line:** `Your care reaches further than your calendar.`

**Use:** Pinned post. Open the webinar with it. Use on the homepage.

**Tone:** Calm, founder-direct, written to be read slowly.

**Visual suggestion:** None. Let the words carry it. Optional quote card at the end using the final line.

**CTA:** Message me if you want to compare notes on what between-session care could look like in your practice.

**Post copy:**

A therapist only gets about thirty minutes a week with a child.

The other one hundred and sixty-seven hours happen without them.

That is not a problem of skill.

It is a problem of reach.

Good goals get set in the session.
They often get lost in the week.

Not because families do not care.
Not because therapists are not doing enough.

Because the care has nowhere to live once the session ends.

I do not think the answer is more therapists doing more hours.

I do not think the answer is automating anyone's clinical judgment away.

I think the answer is reach.

The same therapist. The same plan. The same standard.
Just extended into the week, the home, the waiting period, and the school day, under their supervision.

That is what I keep coming back to.

Therapists change lives in thirty minutes a week.

The work we are doing at Wulo is about making the other one hundred and sixty-seven hours count, under the therapist's plan, in the therapist's voice, on the therapist's terms.

If that is a problem you have lived inside your practice, I would like to compare notes.

## Anchor Post 2 — Continuity

**Rung:** 2 — Continuity of care.

**Spine line:** `The plan you set actually gets practised.`

**Use:** Week 1 cornerstone. Open the webinar invite email. Use as a LinkedIn ad headline.

**Tone:** Observational, quietly frustrated, unmistakably from inside the clinical world.

**Visual suggestion:** 3-slide carousel. Slide 1: `The plan you set actually gets practised.` Slide 2: `Most progress is not lost in the session.` Slide 3: `It is lost in the silence afterwards.` Or a single quote card if posting as text.

**CTA:** Join the next therapist walkthrough — link in comments.

**Post copy:**

There is a quiet failure mode in speech work that almost nobody talks about.

The therapist sets a brilliant plan.

The child leaves the room.

And then the plan goes soft.

Parents mean to practise and do not always know how.
Activities get improvised.
The target gets watered down.
By the next session, half the week has been lost to vague effort.

The therapist walks back in and spends the first ten minutes of a thirty-minute appointment rebuilding what should have been reinforced.

This is not a parent failure.
It is not a therapist failure.
It is a continuity failure.

Most digital tools aimed at therapists have tried to solve this with more content. More worksheets. More apps. More printables.

That is not the problem.

The problem is that the plan you set does not actually get practised the way you would practise it.

That is the gap Wulo is built around.

Guided practice the child can do in short bursts.
Structure the family can follow without guessing.
A review the therapist can trust when they walk back into the room.

One plan. Lived across the week. Under the therapist's supervision, not around it.

If this is a live frustration in your practice, say so. I want to hear how you currently handle it.

## Anchor Post 3 — Mission

**Rung:** 4 — Reach into underserved families.

**Spine line:** `Progress shouldn't depend on privilege.`

**Use:** Week 4 cornerstone. Close the webinar with it. Use on the pilot-invitation page. Use on the founder page.

**Tone:** Slightly slower, more moral, unafraid of the real subject. Still therapist-first.

**Visual suggestion:** Quote card with the first line. Or a founder video clip reading the post straight to camera — this one lands hardest spoken.

**CTA:** If you are a therapist, clinic, or service working with underserved children, I would like to talk about a pilot.

**Post copy:**

Progress shouldn't depend on privilege.

But in child speech work, it often does.

It depends on whether a family can afford private sessions.
On whether a waiting list moves.
On whether a school has a therapist on site.
On whether a parent has time, confidence, and quiet evenings.
On whether anyone can hold the plan together across the week.

The children who have all of that tend to keep moving.

The children who do not, fall behind for reasons that have nothing to do with them.

I do not think the answer is more cheap, thin, unsupervised technology.

I think the answer is therapists, extended further.

The same clinical standard, reaching into homes, schools, waiting periods, and communities it currently cannot reach because there simply are not enough hours in the week.

That is the quiet ambition behind Wulo.

Not to replace therapists.
Not to make therapy cheap.
To make therapist-led progress possible for more of the children who need it.

If you are a therapist, a clinic, or a service working with underserved children and you have ever felt the weight of who you could not reach, I would like to talk.

We are opening a small pilot cohort. Message me.

## Publishing Order

Suggested sequence across a month:

1. **Week 1, Day 2** — publish Anchor Post 2 (Continuity). It earns the diagnosis before any promise.
2. **Week 2, Day 8** — publish Anchor Post 1 (Leverage). It converts the diagnosis into a promise.
3. **Week 4, Day 28** — publish Anchor Post 3 (Mission). It closes on meaning and invites the pilot.

Pin Anchor Post 1 to the founder profile for the duration of the campaign. Rotate Anchor Post 3 into the pin slot in the final week to drive pilot applications.

Each anchor post is a reservoir. Derivative assets to spin from them:

- Homepage hero quote — pull from Anchor 1 final line.
- LinkedIn ad headline — pull from Anchor 2 spine line.
- Webinar title slide — pull from Anchor 3 spine line.
- Sales deck opener — pull from Anchor 1 opening.
- Email newsletter intro — remix Anchor 2 middle section.
- Investor one-liner — Anchor 1 spine plus Anchor 3 close.

Do not rewrite the anchors. Quote them. Let them become the repeated phrases the market starts associating with Wulo.
