# Wulo 30-Day LinkedIn Campaign

## Objective

Generate therapist trust, LinkedIn engagement, webinar registrations, demo requests, and pilot conversations by running a calm, founder-led, therapist-first content program for 30 days.

## Primary Audience

- Independent paediatric SLTs
- Small clinic owners

## Secondary Audience

- School-linked therapists
- SEND and inclusion leads who work closely with therapists
- NHS-adjacent stakeholders watching waiting-list and continuity problems

## Core Campaign Message

`Progress shouldn't depend on privilege.`

Every post should ladder back to a practical therapist-first version of that idea:

`Wulo helps therapists extend structured practice beyond live sessions, so more children keep moving without losing clinical control.`

## Operating Principles

1. Teach the category before pushing the product.
2. Sound like a clinically literate founder, not a growth marketer.
3. Keep every post therapist-first, even when discussing waiting lists or family access.
4. Use access and affordability as continuity and reach, not "cheap therapy."
5. Reuse the same strategic spine across different creative angles.

## Campaign Ladder Mapping

Each week of the campaign is anchored to one rung of the therapist value ladder defined in `01-brand-platform-best-practice.md`. This keeps the narrative ascending rather than scattered.

| Week | Days | Ladder rung | Weekly theme | Emotional job |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Week 1 | 1-7 | Rung 2 — Continuity of care | `The plan you set actually gets practised.` | Name the pain therapists already live with |
| Week 2 | 8-14 | Rung 1 — Professional leverage | `Your care reaches further than your calendar.` | Reframe Wulo as leverage, not replacement |
| Week 3 | 15-21 | Rung 3 — Visibility and evidence | `You see what happened between sessions.` | Provide rational proof and workflow credibility |
| Week 4 | 22-30 | Rung 4 — Reach and mission | `Help the children who would otherwise never see you.` | Close on mission, invite pilot commitment |

Rungs 5 (time) and 6 (income) are never lead messages. They appear only as supporting proof inside demo walkthroughs, case studies, and pricing conversations.

## Weekly Narrative Arc

### Week 1 — Continuity (days 1-7)

Open by naming the week-between gap. This is the shared clinical frustration. Do not pitch. Diagnose. Establish Wulo as a serious observer of the real problem before anyone sees the product.

### Week 2 — Leverage (days 8-14)

Shift from problem to promise. Introduce the idea that the therapist's care can travel further without diluting their standards. This is the emotional pivot from "Wulo sees the problem" to "Wulo extends the therapist."

### Week 3 — Visibility (days 15-21)

Bring the workflow on stage. Show structured review, evidence, follow-through. This is where skeptical therapists and clinic owners get the proof they need to consider a pilot.

### Week 4 — Reach and mission (days 22-30)

Close on the moral frame: `Progress shouldn't depend on privilege.` Tie the therapist's work to the children who would otherwise not be reached. End with a clear pilot invitation.

## Daily Copy Bank

## Day 1 - Progress Shouldn't Depend on Privilege

**Creative angle:** Open with the broad brand truth, then narrow it to therapist-led continuity.

**Suggested format:** Plain text founder post.

**Suggested visual:** None.

**CTA:** Message me if this is a live issue in your practice.

**Post copy:**

Too much progress in child speech work still depends on what happens after the session ends.

Not only on the quality of the target.

On whether families know what to do.
On whether practice feels structured.
On whether a child keeps momentum between appointments.
On whether the therapist can see what actually happened.

That means progress can depend on time, confidence, location, and circumstance more than it should.

It should not.

The question I keep coming back to is simple:

How do we help therapist-led care travel further between sessions without replacing therapist judgment?

That is the gap Wulo is being built around.

If this is a live issue in your practice, I would genuinely like to compare notes.

## Day 3 - Homework Is Not Continuity

**Creative angle:** Create a category distinction.

**Suggested format:** Text post.

**Suggested visual:** None.

**CTA:** Request the pilot overview.

**Post copy:**

There is a big difference between homework and continuity.

Homework says: here is something to try.

Continuity says:

here is what to practice,
here is how support should look,
here is what the child will experience,
and here is what the therapist will be able to review later.

That difference matters more than it sounds.

It shapes follow-through.
It shapes parent confidence.
It shapes what the therapist walks back into at the next appointment.

I do not think most therapists need more tools that create more noise.

I think they need better continuity.

That is the standard I think digital practice tools should be held to.
