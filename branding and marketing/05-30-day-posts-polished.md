# Week 1 — Continuity

**Rung:** Continuity (with mission framing) · **Format:** Text post · **CTA:** Message me if this is live in your practice

Most progress in child speech work is not decided in the session.

It's decided in the week after.

In whether the plan gets practised.
In whether the family knows what to do.
In whether the child keeps momentum before the next appointment.

Which means progress still depends, more than it should, on time, postcode, and how much structure a family can hold together on their own.

I don't think the answer is more cheap, thin, unsupervised tech.

I think the answer is therapists, reaching further.

If that tension is live in your practice, I'd like to compare notes.

## Day 3 — Homework Is Not Continuity

**Rung:** Continuity · **Format:** Text post · **CTA:** Request the pilot overview

There's a difference between homework and continuity, and it matters more than it sounds.

Homework says: here's something to try.

Continuity says: here's what to practise, here's how it should feel, here's what the child will actually experience, and here's what the therapist will be able to review afterwards.

One of those is a worksheet.

The other one moves the target.

Therapists don't need more tools that generate more noise. They need better continuity that shows up the same way every time.

That's the standard I think digital practice tools should be held to.

**Rung:** Continuity · **Format:** Text post + quote card · **CTA:** Join the webinar

## Day 11 — Structured Review, Not Raw Data

**Rung:** Leverage · **Format:** Text post · **CTA:** Request the pilot pack

Therapists don't need more dashboards.

Structured review is a clinical tool, not an analytics tool.

## Day 12 — A Better Practice Loop

**Rung:** Leverage · **Format:** Diagram post · **CTA:** Book a walkthrough

A cleaner loop looks like this:

Therapist sets the plan.
Child practises under structure.
Parent sees exactly what to support.
Therapist reviews what happened.
Next plan is sharper than the last.

No loose ends. No guesswork in the middle. No catch-up in the first ten minutes of the next session.

That loop is what between-session care has been missing. Not more content. Not smarter AI. A closed loop.

Wulo is a closed-loop practice layer.

## Day 23 — Objection: But Can AI Really Do This Safely

**Rung:** Mission (trust) · **Format:** Text post · **CTA:** Ask a question

The honest answer to the safety question is: only if the therapist stays in charge.

No AI should be making clinical decisions for a child.
No AI should be diagnosing.
No AI should be escalating without therapist visibility.
No AI should be deciding when to change a target.

What AI can do, carefully, is guide structured practice the therapist has already set, and surface what happened for the therapist to review.

That boundary isn't a limitation of Wulo. It is Wulo.

If you have harder questions than that, please ask them. They'll make the product better.
