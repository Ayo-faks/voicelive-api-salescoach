# Therapist Demo Sales Deck Narrative

**Deck audience:** Independent paediatric SLTs and small clinic owners  
**Primary objective:** Convert interest into a pilot or walkthrough follow-up  
**Narrative anchor:** So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.

## Deck Strategy

This deck should not feel like a general startup pitch. It should feel like a clinically literate workflow story.

The therapist should leave thinking:

- This solves a real problem in my practice.
- It does not try to replace me.
- I can see how this would fit into my current workflow.
- A pilot feels low-risk and worth testing.

## Slide-by-Slide Narrative

## Slide 1. Cover

**Title:** Wulo  
**Subtitle:** So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.

Talk track:

Wulo is a therapist-supervised speech practice platform designed to help children keep working on targets between sessions while giving therapists clearer review and less follow-up friction.

## Slide 2. The problem therapists already live with

**Title:** The session is not the whole therapy story.

Core points:

- Progress depends on what happens between appointments.
- Home practice is often inconsistent or unclear.
- Parents want to help but need structure.
- Therapists spend too much time catching up instead of moving forward.

Talk track:

The biggest care gap is often not inside the appointment. It is what happens after the child leaves and before they come back.

## Slide 3. What breaks down between sessions

**Title:** Good targets often turn into weak follow-through.

Visual idea:

A simple four-step chain with breakpoints:

1. Therapist sets the target
2. Parent tries to help
3. Child practices inconsistently
4. Therapist has limited visibility

Talk track:

Even when the clinical target is right, the follow-through loop is often fragile.

## Slide 4. The Wulo answer

**Title:** Wulo is the practice layer between sessions.

Core points:

- Therapist sets the practice path
- Child gets guided, structured exercises
- Parent gets a clear supporting role
- Therapist reviews before the next appointment

Talk track:

Wulo is not trying to become the therapist. It is the operating layer that helps therapist-led care continue between sessions.

## Slide 5. What the therapist stays in control of

**Title:** Therapist-led from start to finish.

Core points:

- Goals and targets
- Exercise choice
- Interpretation of progress
- Decisions about next steps

Talk track:

The platform handles delivery and visibility. The therapist remains the decision-maker.

## Slide 6. What gets easier in practice

**Title:** What improves for your workflow.

Core points:

- Less homework chasing
- Faster review before sessions
- Clearer parent follow-through
- More continuity between appointments

Talk track:

The value is not only that children practice. It is that therapists return to structured evidence instead of uncertainty.

## Slide 7. Product walkthrough

**Title:** How Wulo works in a real therapy loop.

Recommended demo sequence:

1. Select the child
2. Choose the exercise and set the practice
3. Show the child-facing guided session
4. Show saved review or dashboard history
5. Show memory, recommendations, or planning if available

Talk track:

Keep this section concrete. Show the workflow, not just the UI.

## Slide 8. Use cases

**Title:** Where therapists are finding the biggest fit.

Three use cases:

- Between-session home practice
- Waiting-list support
- School-linked continuity support

Talk track:

Lead with between-session practice for private therapists, then mention the others as adjacent value.

## Slide 9. Trust, boundaries, and safeguarding

**Title:** Built for therapist-supervised care.

Core points:

- Not diagnosis
- Not treatment replacement
- Therapist-led workflows
- Privacy and safeguarding expectations respected

Talk track:

Trust matters more than novelty in this category. Address this directly.

## Slide 10. Pilot offer

**Title:** What a pilot looks like.

Recommended structure:

- Best for independent therapists or small clinics
- Start with a small child cohort
- Test guided between-session practice over a short period
- Capture therapist feedback, parent friction points, and adherence signal

Talk track:

Make the pilot feel practical, time-bounded, and collaborative.

## Slide 11. Why now

**Title:** Children cannot afford dead time between sessions.

Core points:

- Caseload pressure is high
- Waiting times are long
- Families want something concrete to do
- Therapists need leverage, not replacement

Talk track:

Wulo exists because one hour a week is rarely enough on its own.

## Slide 12. Close

**Title:** Keep care working between sessions.

CTA options:

- Start a pilot
- Book a therapist walkthrough
- Join the next design-partner cohort

Closing talk track:

If the between-session gap is a live issue in your practice, Wulo is built to help you close it without giving up therapist control.

## Demo Presenter Notes

### Best opening question

`What usually happens to your targets between one session and the next?`

### Best discovery questions

- How do you currently handle home practice?
- What do parents find hardest to follow through on?
- How do you know what actually happened between appointments?
- What part of review feels most manual today?

### Objection handling

**If they ask whether this replaces therapy:**
No. It extends therapist-led care and gives the therapist better continuity and visibility.

**If they ask whether parents can use it alone:**
It is built for therapist-supervised use, with structure and boundaries rather than open-ended self-direction.

**If they ask whether this creates more admin:**
The design goal is the opposite: less chasing, faster review, and clearer context before the next session.

## Suggested deck design direction

- Keep the design warm, clinical, and calm.
- Use one practical workflow screenshot per major section.
- Prefer real therapist scenarios over abstract product claims.
