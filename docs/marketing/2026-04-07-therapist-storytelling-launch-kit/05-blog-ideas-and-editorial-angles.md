# Blog Ideas and Editorial Angles

**Purpose:** Build therapist trust, improve SEO around therapist-led practice support, and create assets that can be reused in outbound outreach.

## Editorial Direction

The blog should not sound like generic startup marketing. It should sound like a thoughtful partner to therapists working under time and continuity pressure.

The strongest editorial territory is:

- between-session care
- parent follow-through
- waiting-list support
- therapist workflow efficiency
- clinical boundaries in digital support tools

## Content Formula for Each Blog Post

Use this structure repeatedly:

1. Name the real-world therapist problem.
2. Explain why current workflows break down.
3. Show the cost to child progress, parents, and therapist time.
4. Introduce a better model.
5. Close with a low-pressure invitation to talk or test.

## Proof Assets to Gather for Future Blog Strength

- therapist quotes from discovery calls
- early pilot observations
- anonymized parent follow-through patterns
- simple workflow screenshots
- adherence or review-time signals from pilots
