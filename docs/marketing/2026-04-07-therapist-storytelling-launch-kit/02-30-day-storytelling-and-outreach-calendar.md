# 30-Day Storytelling and Outreach Calendar

**Start date:** April 7, 2026  
**Primary goal:** Start GTM motion immediately, recruit pilot conversations, and build therapist-market signal  
**Primary audience:** Independent paediatric SLTs and small clinics  
**Secondary audience:** School-facing therapists and SENCO-linked practices

## Operating Principles

- Keep the message centered on therapist-led care between sessions.
- Use founder-led outreach in parallel with public storytelling.
- Tie content to the ASLTIP outreach segments already prepared in `docs/prospecting`.
- Publish less, but make every asset reusable in outreach.

## Weekly Themes

- **Week 1:** Name the problem better than the market.
- **Week 2:** Show the therapist workflow and practical value.
- **Week 3:** Build trust, boundaries, and pilot credibility.
- **Week 4:** Push direct pilot conversion and proof gathering.
- **Days 29-30:** Consolidate, follow up, and publish a stronger point of view.

## Daily Execution Priorities

Every day should include three motions:

1. One outward-facing story asset
2. One set of direct outreach actions
3. One learning capture step

## Recommended Outreach Sequence for the First 10 Days

### Segment order

1. Telehealth continuity practices
2. Articulation and DLD carryover clinics
3. School-facing and parent-reporting practices

### Why this order

The telehealth continuity group is most likely to feel the between-session gap acutely and respond quickly to the Wulo story.

## Key Metrics to Track During the 30 Days

- Outbound emails sent
- Positive reply rate
- Pilot conversations booked
- Landing-page CTA clicks
- LinkedIn post saves and replies
- Webinar registrations
- Requests for walkthroughs
- Objections mentioned most often

## Fastest Wins

If time is constrained, prioritize these five items first:

1. Founder LinkedIn post on April 7
2. First 15 ASLTIP emails in the first three days
3. A simple therapist walkthrough asset
4. A pilot overview one-pager
5. A therapist webinar invitation before the end of week three
