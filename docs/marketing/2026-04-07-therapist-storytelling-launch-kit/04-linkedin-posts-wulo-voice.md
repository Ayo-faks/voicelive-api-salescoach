# 10 LinkedIn Posts Written in Wulo's Voice

**Audience:** Speech and language therapists, clinic owners, school-linked therapists, and SENCO-adjacent professionals  
**Voice:** Calm, credible, therapist-first, practical  
**Primary CTA pattern:** Invite conversation or pilot interest, not hard sell

## Post 1. The between-session truth

The real challenge in speech therapy is not only what happens in the session.

It is what happens after.

A therapist can set the right goal, explain it clearly, and send home the best intentions in the world. But if practice is vague, inconsistent, or hard for parents to support, momentum fades fast.

That is the gap we are building Wulo for.

Not to replace the therapist. To help care keep working between sessions.

If that is a live problem in your practice, I would like to compare notes.

## Post 2. Parents do not need more advice

Parents are usually not asking for more theory.

They are asking:

What exactly should we do this week?
How often?
How do I help without doing it wrong?

One reason home practice breaks down is that families are given instructions, but not enough structure.

Wulo is built around that reality: guided child practice, a clearer role for the parent, and better visibility for the therapist.

Because `try to practise this at home` is rarely enough on its own.

## Post 3. Therapists do not need more noise

Therapists do not need more raw data.

They need a faster way to understand what happened between sessions.

Who practiced?
What was hard?
Where did the child stall?
What is ready to move on?

The value of a practice platform is not how much it records.

It is whether it helps the therapist start the next session with insight instead of catch-up.

That is the standard we think matters.

## Post 4. One hour a week is rarely enough

Children need more than one hour a week.

Not more pressure. Not more confusion.

More continuity.

That is why we think the biggest opportunity in therapist-led care is the space between appointments.

If that space becomes structured, supported, and reviewable, the therapist gets leverage without giving up judgment.

That is the promise behind Wulo.

So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.

## Post 5. What waiting-list families actually need

For many families, the hardest part of the waiting list is not only the delay.

It is the silence.

No clear routine.
No guided activities.
No sense of momentum.

We think there is room for something better: supervised, non-diagnostic practice that gives families something concrete to do while therapist-led care is still pending or just beginning.

That is one of the reasons Wulo exists.

## Post 6. The therapist remains the decision-maker

Every time a digital tool enters a clinical workflow, the same question should be asked:

Who stays in control?

Our view is simple.

The therapist sets the goals.
The therapist decides what matters.
The therapist interprets progress.
The therapist chooses next steps.

The platform should support that work, not compete with it.

That principle shapes how we think about Wulo.

## Post 7. Homework is not the same as structured care

There is a big difference between home practice and structured between-session care.

Homework says: here is something to try.

Structured care says: here is what to practice, how often, what support looks like, and what the therapist will be able to review.

That difference matters.

It changes follow-through.
It changes therapist visibility.
It changes how the next session starts.

We think that is where a lot of progress is won or lost.

## Post 8. What a therapist pilot should feel like

A good pilot should not feel like extra work disguised as innovation.

It should feel like this:

- easy to start
- clear about boundaries
- small enough to test properly
- focused on real workflow value

That is how we are approaching Wulo pilots with therapists.

The goal is not to prove a big vision in theory.

The goal is to see whether this actually helps in real practice.

## Post 9. The question we keep coming back to

What usually happens to a child's targets between one appointment and the next?

That question is shaping almost everything we are building.

Because if the answer is `it depends`, `we are not sure`, or `parents try when they can`, then the therapy loop is carrying too much uncertainty.

Wulo is our attempt to make that loop more structured, more visible, and more useful to the therapist.
