# Wulo Therapist Pilot Overview

**For independent speech and language therapists and small clinics**

## Keep care working between sessions

**So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.**

Wulo is a therapist-supervised speech practice platform that helps children continue guided work between appointments, gives parents a clearer role at home, and gives therapists structured review before the next session.

Wulo does **not** replace diagnosis, treatment, or therapist judgment. It supports therapist-led care by strengthening what happens between sessions.

## Why therapists are interested

Many therapists already know the problem:

- children need more than one hour a week
- home practice is often inconsistent
- parents want to help but need structure
- the next session can start with catch-up instead of progress

Wulo is designed to make between-session care more structured, more visible, and easier to review.

## What the pilot is for

The Wulo pilot is designed to test whether therapist-led guided practice can:

- improve follow-through between sessions
- give parents a clearer support role
- reduce homework chasing and manual review friction
- help therapists start the next session with better context

## What Wulo gives you

- therapist-directed guided practice for children
- a clearer home-practice loop for families
- structured progress review before the next appointment
- a practical way to test continuity of care between sessions

## What stays under therapist control

- therapy goals and targets
- exercise selection
- interpretation of progress
- next-step decisions

Wulo handles the practice layer. The therapist remains the decision-maker.

## Suggested pilot structure

**Ideal fit:** Independent paediatric SLTs and small clinics  
**Suggested cohort:** 3 to 10 children  
**Suggested duration:** 4 to 6 weeks  
**Best use case:** Between-session practice for children who need more consistent carryover between appointments

## What we want to learn with you

- Where do families follow through well and where do they stall?
- What kind of review is most useful before the next session?
- What reduces friction for therapists?
- What helps the platform fit naturally into real practice?

## Pilot success signals

- families complete practice more consistently
- therapists can review progress more quickly
- therapists feel they have better visibility between sessions
- children arrive with more continuity from prior work

## What a pilot should feel like

This should feel:

- practical, not theoretical
- low-friction, not admin-heavy
- therapist-led, not platform-led
- collaborative, with clear feedback loops

## Best-fit conversations

The strongest fit is usually with therapists who are seeing one or more of these issues:

- low home-practice adherence
- unclear parent follow-through
- limited visibility between appointments
- too much catching up at the start of sessions
