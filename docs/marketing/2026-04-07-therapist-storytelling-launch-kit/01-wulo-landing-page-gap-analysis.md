# Wulo Landing Page Gap Analysis

**Page reviewed:** https://www.wulo.ai/  
**Review date:** April 7, 2026  
**Review method:** Browser audit using the local browser tool and page snapshot

## Executive Summary

The current Wulo landing page is directionally strong. It already expresses the right market position: therapist-supervised practice, not therapist replacement. The page also frames the core operational gap well: what happens between sessions.

The main issue is not positioning failure. The issue is conversion dilution.

Today the page explains Wulo well, but it does not yet convert as hard as it could for the current go-to-market motion. It needs a more focused primary buyer path, stronger proof, a clearer pilot ask, and sharper translation from product features into therapist outcomes.

## What Is Working

### 1. The strategic story is correct

The page consistently positions Wulo as the practice layer between sessions. That is the strongest market story for the product.

### 2. The therapist boundary is well handled

The page repeatedly states that Wulo does not replace the therapist. This is important for trust and should remain.

### 3. The problem framing is credible

The waiting-list statistics, therapist shortage context, and between-session care gap create a strong reason to care.

### 4. The use-case framing is commercially useful

The three use cases are sensible:

- Between-session practice
- Waiting-list support
- School support layer

These can become separate campaign tracks over time.

### 5. The page already contains therapist-relevant language

Phrases such as `structured review`, `without extra admin`, and `therapist-controlled` align well with the buyer psychology in the ASLTIP prospecting materials.

## Core Gaps

### Gap 1. Too many buyer stories compete on one page

The page speaks to therapists, schools, waiting-list pathways, parents, local authorities, and NHS stakeholders all at once. That is strategically understandable, but for immediate GTM it weakens conversion.

**Impact:** Independent SLTs and small clinics are the fastest buyers, but the page does not quite privilege them enough as the primary action path.

**Recommendation:**

- Make independent therapists and clinics the dominant homepage conversion story.
- Keep school and public-sector stories lower on the page as future expansion signals.
- Add a section explicitly labeled `For independent therapists and clinics` near the hero.

### Gap 2. The hero is strong on message but light on proof

The current hero communicates the idea well, but it still reads as strategic positioning rather than proof-backed conversion copy.

**Impact:** A therapist may agree with the story but still ask, `What exactly gets easier for me tomorrow?`

**Recommendation:**

- Keep the headline direction.
- Add a short proof bar beneath the CTA with claims such as:
  - Guided home practice set by the therapist
  - Structured review before the next session
  - Parent support without guesswork
- Add one line of operational specificity under the hero: `Assign targets in minutes. Review practice before the next appointment.`

### Gap 3. The page lacks visible product proof

The current page talks about the product but does not yet show enough of it.

**Impact:** Buyers cannot quickly picture the workflow or trust that the product is real, usable, and therapist-friendly.

**Recommendation:**

- Add screenshots or annotated UI panels for:
  - Therapist home
  - Session review dashboard
  - Recommendations and plan workflow
- Add a simple 30 to 45 second demo clip or GIF.
- Show the therapist workflow in three frames: assign, child practices, therapist reviews.

### Gap 4. There is not enough social proof yet

The page has trust language but limited credibility artifacts.

**Impact:** Therapists may see a thoughtful platform, but not enough evidence that peers trust it.

**Recommendation:**

- Add pilot language such as `Now onboarding design-partner therapists`.
- Add one founder note or clinical advisory statement if available.
- Add early testimonial placeholders as soon as pilot feedback exists.
- Add a small `built with therapist input` proof strip if accurate.

### Gap 5. The CTA path is too generic for the current sales motion

`Start a Pilot` is a good CTA, but it is doing too much on its own.

**Impact:** Cold traffic often needs a lower-friction step before a pilot commitment.

**Recommendation:**

- Keep `Start a Pilot` as the primary CTA.
- Add a secondary CTA such as:
  - `Book a 20-minute therapist walkthrough`
  - `See the therapist workflow`
  - `Get the pilot overview`
- Add a small pilot explainer directly below the CTA: `Best for independent SLTs and small clinics testing guided between-session practice with 3 to 10 children.`

### Gap 6. The page hints at outcomes but does not quantify them

The copy says Wulo reduces admin and improves continuity, but it does not yet present measurable outcome categories.

**Impact:** The value sounds plausible but not fully commercial.

**Recommendation:**

- Add an outcomes section framed around therapist value:
  - Faster review before appointments
  - Better adherence between sessions
  - Clearer parent follow-through
  - More consistent continuity of care
- Once pilots begin, replace qualitative claims with pilot metrics.

### Gap 7. The page does not fully exploit the strongest emotional truth

The most compelling idea is that care should keep working after the session ends. The page is close to this story, but it is not yet using it as the emotional through-line everywhere.

**Impact:** The brand story feels strategically clear but not yet memorable enough.

**Recommendation:**

- Use the payoff line consistently: `So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.`
- Introduce that idea in the hero, again in the therapist section, and once more near the final CTA.

## Recommended Homepage Messaging Structure

## 1. Hero

**Headline:** The practice layer for care between sessions.  
**Support line:** So your care keeps working, even when the session ends.  
**Body:** Wulo helps speech and language therapists assign guided practice, involve parents clearly, and review progress before the next appointment without extra admin.

Primary CTA: `Start a Pilot`  
Secondary CTA: `Book a Therapist Walkthrough`

## 2. Immediate proof strip

- Therapist-directed
- Guided child practice
- Structured review
- Parent support built in

## 3. Workflow proof

Show the actual loop:

1. Therapist sets goals and practice.
2. Child practices between sessions.
3. Parent supports with structure.
4. Therapist reviews with context.

## 4. Therapist outcome section

Headline: `What gets easier for therapists`

- Less homework chasing
- Faster review before sessions
- Better continuity between appointments
- More visible follow-through from families

## 5. Pilot credibility section

Headline: `Built for therapist-led pilots`

Include:

- who the pilot is for
- what happens during a pilot
- how long it takes to get started
- what feedback you want from therapists

## 6. Final CTA

Use the emotional line again.

Example:

`Children need more than one hour a week. Wulo helps your care keep working in between.`

## Conversion Recommendations by Priority

### Priority 1. Tighten for one ICP

Make independent paediatric SLTs and clinics the homepage priority for the next 30 days.

### Priority 2. Add product proof

Screenshots, a demo clip, and a workflow graphic should be added before broad outreach scales.

### Priority 3. Add pilot clarity

Spell out who the pilot is for, what happens, and how low-friction the first step is.

### Priority 4. Add proof artifacts

Use therapist input, early case feedback, or advisory credibility to reduce perceived risk.

### Priority 5. Strengthen the emotional brand line

Use the line about care continuing after the session as a repeated campaign anchor.

## Final Assessment

Wulo does not need a landing-page rewrite from scratch. It needs a conversion hardening pass.

The foundation is already strong. The next step is to make the page behave more like a therapist pilot-conversion page and less like a broad market narrative page.
