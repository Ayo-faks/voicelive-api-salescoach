# Wulo Competitor Matrix

Generated: 2026-04-05

## Purpose

This matrix compares Wulo against public, relevant products in pediatric speech practice, assistive communication, and adjacent digital therapy. The goal is not to claim Wulo is better at everything. The goal is to show where Wulo has a distinct wedge.

## Wulo's working position

Wulo is strongest when positioned as:

- therapist-supervised pediatric speech practice;
- structured between-session continuity;
- child-friendly guided voice interaction;
- therapist review and workflow support;
- home and supervised practice, not autonomous therapy.

## Where Wulo wins if executed well

Wulo's clearest advantages are not "AI" in the abstract. They are:

1. therapist-supervised design rather than a child-only consumer app;
2. between-session continuity rather than isolated exercises;
3. structured therapist review rather than only parent progress summaries;
4. a clinically safer position because the product explicitly supports practice and does not replace diagnosis or treatment planning;
5. a product story that can speak to private therapists, teletherapy providers, and eventually NHS or school-linked pathways.

## Where Wulo is still weaker today

These are the likely weak spots compared with established products:

1. brand recognition and trust;
2. public clinical evidence;
3. installed base of therapists or institutions;
4. breadth of polished content library;
5. depth in adjacent modalities such as AAC or autism-specific support.

## Strategic takeaway

Wulo should avoid competing as just another speech app. That is a crowded and weaker position.

The better position is:

Wulo is the therapist-controlled practice layer that sits between live sessions and real-world child follow-through.

That wedge is stronger because it aligns with:

1. grant language around access, outcomes, and workforce efficiency;
2. therapist pain around continuity and review burden;
3. parent pain around not knowing what to do between sessions;
4. investor interest in measurable workflow and outcomes, not only content.

## Practical messages Wulo can use

Against consumer speech apps:

- Wulo is not just engaging practice. It is therapist-supervised, reviewable practice.

Against older articulation apps:

- Wulo is not just a library. It is a structured practice-and-review workflow.

Against broad SEN or AAC tools:

- Wulo is not trying to be every communication tool. It is focused on supervised speech practice continuity.

Against generic AI tutors:

- Wulo is clinically bounded and built for therapist-led use, not freeform autonomous coaching.
