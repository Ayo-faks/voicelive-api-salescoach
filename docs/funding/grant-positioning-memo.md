## Purpose

This memo gives Wulo a reusable funding narrative for:

1. NIHR i4i
2. SBRI Healthcare
3. MIT Solve

The core principle is consistency. Wulo should present the same underlying truth across all three, then shift emphasis based on reviewer priorities.

## Core positioning statement

Wulo is a therapist-supervised pediatric speech practice platform that helps children with speech and language needs receive more structured, engaging, and measurable practice between live therapy sessions. It gives therapists guided exercise delivery, pronunciation-informed review, and clearer visibility into progress outside appointments, helping families and clinicians turn recommendations into monitored follow-through.

## The problem Wulo solves

Children who need speech and language support often face a practical gap between sessions:

1. live therapist time is scarce;
2. home practice is inconsistent;
3. therapists have limited visibility into what happens outside appointments;
4. caregivers may not know how to support practice confidently;
5. progress can stall because the period between sessions is under-structured.

Wulo addresses this gap without claiming to replace diagnosis, clinical judgment, or therapist-led intervention planning.

## What makes Wulo fundable

Wulo is stronger as a funding story when described as:

- access-enabling, because it extends structured support beyond scarce live sessions;
- workforce-supporting, because it helps therapists review and manage practice more efficiently;
- child-centered, because it makes practice more engaging and structured for children;
- measurable, because usage, adherence, and review activity can be tracked;
- health-relevant, because speech-language support affects communication, development, participation, and care access.

## What not to lead with

Do not lead with:

1. technical stack details;
2. Azure or model names;
3. vague "AI-powered" claims;
4. any implication that Wulo provides autonomous therapy;
5. a generic edtech narrative detached from therapist supervision and care delivery.

## Reusable short form answers

## One-sentence version

Wulo is a therapist-supervised speech practice platform that helps children with speech and language needs receive more structured, engaging, and measurable practice between therapy sessions.

## Two-sentence version

Wulo helps children with speech and language needs practice more consistently between live therapy sessions through guided, child-friendly voice interactions and therapist-authored exercises. It also gives therapists clearer visibility into progress and follow-through, helping families and clinicians turn recommendations into monitored practice.

## Problem statement version

Too much pediatric speech and language support depends on what happens between appointments, yet therapists often lack lightweight tools to structure home or supervised practice and to review what actually happened outside the session. This weak continuity slows progress, increases burden on families, and makes scarce specialist time less effective.

## Why now version

The need for scalable, therapist-supported practice is rising as specialist capacity remains limited, children with SEN need more personalized support, and families increasingly expect digital tools that can extend care without replacing clinicians. Voice and AI technologies now make it possible to support structured practice and review in ways that were previously too manual or too expensive.

## Scale pathway version

Wulo can scale through private speech-language therapy practices, teletherapy providers, school-linked services, and eventually NHS or community pathways where structured between-session support can improve outcomes and use specialist time more efficiently.

## Evidence narrative for early stage

If evidence is still early, use this frame:

Wulo is at an early but credible stage of development, with a clear product workflow, therapist-facing review model, and a focused target user group in pediatric speech practice. The next funding step is intended to generate the usability, adherence, workflow, and implementation evidence required for broader clinical and commercial adoption.

## Final guidance

Across NIHR, SBRI, and MIT Solve, Wulo should remain the same product in the story.

The emphasis changes like this:

1. NIHR: evidence, adoption pathway, patient-focused digital health.
2. SBRI: NHS problem, implementation, commercial strategy.
3. MIT Solve: human impact, equity, scalable innovation.

If the narrative starts sounding like a generic AI tutor, it is off track.

If it sounds like a clinician-supported practice layer that improves continuity, access, and measurable follow-through for children with speech-language needs, it is on track.
