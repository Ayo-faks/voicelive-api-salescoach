- unvalidated buying behavior

This meeting should therefore **not** spend most of its time on broad ideation about what Wulo might be. The main open questions are now:

- who owns what

## 4. Current Product Story To Use Externally

This is the version of the story that seems most aligned with the product as it exists now.

**Short version:**  
Wulo helps speech and language therapists extend therapy between sessions with therapist-controlled AI practice, simple review dashboards, and clearer next-step planning.

**Boundary statement:**  
Wulo is a practice and review tool. It does not replace diagnosis, treatment, or therapist judgement.

**Simple AI and analytics explanation:**

- AI helps guide practice and organize what happened in the session.
- Analytics means a simple view of what the child attempted, where they retried, and what may need review.
- Real-time review means the therapist gets usable signals faster without listening back to everything.

## 5. What Is Still Assumption vs What Is Known

This section matters because the TAM brief makes clear that Wulo still needs to convert assumptions into evidence.

### What appears strong already

- the problem framing is credible
- the therapist-first positioning is coherent
- the product surface is substantial enough for real pilot use
- there is a plausible wedge with independent SLTs and small clinics

### What is still unproven

- which exact workflow feature creates strongest willingness to pay
- whether outbound will outperform inbound in the first 60 to 90 days, and by how much
- what pricing model therapists accept most naturally
- which objections most frequently block pilot conversion
- which parts of the current feature set are actually core in repeated use

### Why this matters for the founder discussion

The company should now be organized around learning fast from pilots. So the meeting should not only divide functions; it should divide responsibility for validating assumptions.
